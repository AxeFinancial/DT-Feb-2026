R&D Note
Capitalized vs. Expensed

R&D costs of $59,713 (primarily R&D Contractor) are currently expensed — reducing taxable income each period.

If capitalized: These costs would appear as an Intangible Asset on the balance sheet, amortized over the useful life. Net loss would be materially lower, EBITDA would improve, and the business would look more fundable to investors.

If pursuing investors: Financials would need to be restated to capitalize. No issue doing this — it's a common startup adjustment.

Current stance: Expensed (tax basis). Jamie to confirm whether to continue or capitalize going forward.

The W-2 Treadmill — Why Break-Even Isn't a Fixed Number
How it works
Each new client requires dispatcher hours. At 20 hrs/wk per dispatcher and 90% utilization, one dispatcher covers ~4 clients. As you add the 5th client, you hire another dispatcher — adding ~$420/mo in non-billable pay + FICA regardless of revenue.
The net GP per client
Each $1,800 client generates $630 GP (35% margin). After allocating the W-2 burden across the clients that triggered a new dispatcher, net contribution per client is roughly $525/mo. This is what actually closes the gap.
The result
Break-even requires ~84 additional clients (86 total) at $1,800 dispatcher revenue — not because the math is simple, but because each step forward adds a step of cost. Subscription and technology revenue is modeled as a separate layer. The interactive model shows how changing dispatcher hours, utilization, or pay rate shifts this target.

Jamie — please confirm: continue expensing for now, or capitalize going forward?

The subscription product adds $24/mo net GP per client at current pricing; Ron's time-per-subscriber is the key variable to manage.
